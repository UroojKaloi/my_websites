Badshahi Masjid

History of Badshahi Masjid

The Badshahi Masjid in Thatta was built in 1647 by the Mughal Emperor Shah Jahan during his reign. The mosque is a stunning example of Mughal-era architecture and is famous for its intricate tile work, red brick construction, and Persian influences. The mosque has 93 domes, making it the largest domed mosque in Pakistan. The exceptional acoustics allow a person speaking at one end to be heard clearly at the other end without any amplification. The mosque was originally meant to be a gift from Shah Jahan to the people of Sindh and was constructed with locally available red bricks and glazed blue tiles imported from Hala. It remains one of the finest examples of Islamic architecture in South Asia and is a UNESCO Tentative World Heritage Site.
